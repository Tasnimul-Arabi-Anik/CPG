# Manuscript Outline

## Working Title

A comparative-genomics framework for receptor-binding and counter-defense modules shaping Klebsiella phage host range.

## Core Claim

Klebsiella phage host range is shaped by receptor compatibility and intracellular defense/counter-defense compatibility, so taxonomy alone is insufficient for host-range prediction.

## Draft Structure

1. Introduction
   - Clinical and ecological importance of Klebsiella phages.
   - Limits of taxonomy-only phage comparison.
   - Two-layer compatibility hypothesis.

2. Results
   - Curated and dereplicated Klebsiella phage atlas.
   - Phage pangenome and accessory module landscape.
   - RBP/depolymerase structural module catalog.
   - Host K/O/ST/AMR/virulence association analysis.
   - Host defense and phage counter-defense compatibility model.
   - Novel candidate prioritization for experimental follow-up.

3. Discussion
   - Biological interpretation of receptor and defense filters.
   - Prophages as a reservoir of capsule-recognition diversity.
   - Translational implications for phage discovery and cocktail design.
   - Limitations, claim boundaries, and required experimental validation.

4. Methods
   - Dataset curation.
   - QC, dereplication, and genome similarity.
   - Annotation and pangenome analysis.
   - RBP/depolymerase detection.
   - Host feature integration.
   - Defense/counter-defense annotation.
   - Statistical modeling and figure generation.


5. Claim Control
   - Use `docs/claim_ledger.md` before drafting abstract, results, or discussion claims.
   - Keep receptor specificity, infection success, and therapeutic recommendations framed as computational prioritization unless experimental evidence is added.
